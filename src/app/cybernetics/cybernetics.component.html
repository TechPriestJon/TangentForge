CYBERNETICS

I have an interest in Robotics and Cybernetics. My degree was in Artificial Intelligence and Cybernetics and so its something that has always been a passion.

At University there were many who were looking into the science of Cybernetic limbs to help those who had lost their originals due to accident or other reasons. This area is a resource on recent advancements in the field, plus any other ideas I might have regarding cybernetics.

One of my previous Professors, Prof. Kevin Warwick, implanted a chip into his arm which could read his nerve impulses and signals. This was then translated to a robotic hand which moved in the same motion as his real hand. The really fun bit was when he put pressure sensors on the hand and fed the response signals back into his own nervous system. He could feel when the sensors on the robot hand were pressed.

I would love to start a business one day dealing in Cybernetic parts. I see it as an inevitable direction, and whilst there will be a rival in the biology/organic field for limb and organ replacement, I feel a mechanical solution is more reliable.

The interchanging of parts on a case by case basis its sensible. The human form becomes a lego set so you can become whatever you desire to be, unlimited or constrained by nature. Of course the brain would have to adapt to this, but the brain is surprisingly flexible and malleable in this regard.

We will all grow old one day and our organic limbs will fail us. When that day comes I hope the science of cybernetics will be advanced enough to preserve and sustain me far beyond a historic human life span so I can reach the stars which I love so much.

A silly dream, but I would love nothing more than to visit distant stars and worlds, see the birth of planets, the death of moons and the unimaginable life that teems beyond our solar system.

But first we must master the fusion of machine and humanity so we can clutch the stars in our hands.
