MACHINE LEARNING

Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence has been suffering a degree of buzzword syndrome but that seems to be levelling out but we still have a way to go. There are also a massive amount of misconceptions about AI in the general public due to science fiction media.

I disagree with Mr Elon Musk that AI is a great threat to humanity. I believe human level AI is still decades if not centuries away. My reasoning for is -

A) Asking the average Neuron Surgeon if we understand the brain entirely and they will say no, we understand parts of it, we know roughly what regions of the brain do, but certainly not the entirety, and definitely not to neuron level. How can we replicate something we don't understand? We can't.

B) Right now the average 'AI' is a very Weak AI, a term which means not complex. There is no way any AI is approaching a General AI level, everything is very specific in what it does. Yes we can build pieces together, like visual AI which can recognise objects, or facial recognition AI which can recognise eyes, noses and mouths and use that to build an understand of a face, or language AIs which can recognise patterns in language. But recognising is not the same as understanding.

Its the Chinese Room problem. An AI can receive data in, process it and push data out, but that processing is not the same as understanding. It cannot create something new. Even AI that create 'new' works of art, what they create is a randomised version of the data it has been trained with. There is nothing abstract that the AI can take forward, if something was not included in the training data it cannot conceive of it on its own. True Strong AI must be able to abstractly connect concepts like a human can, which I believe is still a long way away as we don't understand how humans do it yet.

An AI trained to recognise pictures of Dogs cannot recognise a stop sign or what it means. It's outside its understanding. Same as an AI trained for cars knows nothing about the Stock Market, nor can it. It can't draw any connections any more than a hamster can appreciate the works of Shakespeare.
